Chapter 2: Convex Function

A convex optimization problem is of the form:
min⁡x∈Df(x) \min _{x \in D} f(x) x∈Dmin​f(x)
subject to
gi(x)≤0,i=1,…,mhj(x)=0,j=1,…,r \begin{array}{l} g_{i}(x) \leq 0, \quad i=1, \ldots, m \\ h_{j}(x)=0, \quad j=1, \ldots, r \end{array} gi​(x)≤0,i=1,…,mhj​(x)=0,j=1,…,r​
where fff and gig_{i}gi​ are all convex functions, and hjh_{j}hj​ are affine functions. DDD is the intersection of the domains of definition of all functions.

Convex optimization problem always has a solution.
Any local minimizer of a convex optimization problem is a global minimizer.
